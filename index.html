Biography

I am currently a Computer Science PhD candidate in the Department of Computer Science and Software Engineering at Concordia University (one of the top 100 universities under 50 years in the world).

My research as a member of the Ambient Software Engineering Group (ASEG) is mainly focused on improving and supporting software engineering tasks using semantic analysis of software build systems through semantic web techniques.

I was born in Ghana where I earned my undergrad degree at the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science & Technology. I later moved to Canada to study at Concordia University, completing my MSc in 2013 and currently pursuing my PhD.

For more information you can download my Curriculum Vitae.
